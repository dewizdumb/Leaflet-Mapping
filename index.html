Visualization of Earthquakes From the Past Week (Real Time)

Here we visualize earthquakes around the world in the past week. The data is updated every minute from the United States Geological Survey (USGS) . I used Leaflet to plot the data, with several layers of maps (including an outdoors map, a dark mode map, and a satellite map). A legend below indicates the color spread of the earthquakes, with weak tremors highlighted in green/yellow, to large quakes colored in deeper reds. The radius of each circle also indicates the magnitude of each earthquake, but there is a caveat. Since I've used tiles of a mercator map, this means earthquakes nearer the poles appear to have a larger radius but a lighter color. This is an artifact of the linear stretching that occurs at the poles. Better tiles will get rid of this artifact, something I may look into at a later point. Either way, click on each point to get more information about the earthquakes magnitude, location, and time.
